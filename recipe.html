Tonkotsu Delight

January 14
Japanese Pork Medium Cozy Indulgent

A rich, creamy pork bone broth that's simmered for 12 hours, topped with chashu pork, soft-boiled egg, and green onions.

Milky white broth swirls
Pork bones whisper their secrets
Comfort in a bowl

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork bones
  • 1 onion, halved
  • 4 cloves garlic
  • 1-inch piece ginger
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon mirin
  • 4 portions ramen noodles
  • 4 soft-boiled eggs
  • 4 slices chashu pork belly
  • Green onions, sliced
  • Nori sheets
  • Bamboo shoots

Preparation

  1. In a large pot, add pork bones and cover with cold water. Bring to a boil and simmer for 5 minutes.
  2. Drain and rinse the bones. Clean the pot.
  3. Return bones to the pot with onion, garlic, and ginger. Cover with water and bring to a boil.
  4. Reduce heat and simmer for 8-12 hours, skimming regularly.
  5. Strain the broth and return to the pot. Add sesame oil, soy sauce, and mirin.
  6. Cook ramen noodles according to package instructions.
  7. Assemble bowls with noodles, broth, chashu, egg, green onions, nori, and bamboo shoots.
